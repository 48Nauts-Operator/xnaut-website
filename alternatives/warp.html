A Warp alternative without the account

Open source · no login · no credits · updated July 2026

Warp is a genuinely good terminal — and since April 2026 its client is even open source (AGPL). What sends people looking for alternatives is everything around it: AI features gated behind an account, a $20/month credit system for the AI you actually want, and telemetry that's on by default.

xNAUT takes the opposite bet: a native 20 MB terminal (Rust + Tauri) where the AI runs on your machine — Ollama or LM Studio on localhost by default — with no account, no credits, and no telemetry at all. MIT-licensed, free forever.

When Warp is the better choice

Honesty ranks better than hype: if you want team-level cloud agent orchestration (Warp's Oz), shared workflows across an org, or a large company behind your terminal, Warp earns its subscription. xNAUT is built for individuals and small teams who want everything local.

Switching takes a minute

xNAUT imports Warp themes (YAML) directly, snippets live in searchable cards, and your shell config is untouched — it's your same zsh/bash/fish underneath. Download xNAUT for macOS or Windows, free.
